Travel to Brazil

Discover Lençóis Maranhenses!

A desert that floods with life

Hidden in Brazil’s northeast, Lençóis Maranhenses National Park defies expectation. Vast white sand dunes ripple toward the horizon, yet between them lie crystal-clear freshwater lagoons, formed by seasonal rains and glowing in shades of turquoise and jade. It’s surreal, elemental, and quietly unforgettable.

Why Lençóis Maranhenses is unlike anywhere else

There’s no rush here. The landscape sets the pace.

This is a place that resists easy categories. Not desert, not coast, not rainforest, but something entirely its own. Days here are shaped by light, wind, and water. You walk barefoot across warm sand, then slip into cool lagoons as if nature planned the contrast just for you.

What you’ll see

  • Endless sculpted dunes shifting with the wind
  • Freshwater lagoons so clear you can see the sand ripple beneath
  • Flamingos and migratory birds tracing the horizon
  • Golden sunsets that turn the dunes rose-pink
  • Star-filled skies untouched by city light

Top experiences that stay with you

  1. Swim Between the Dunes
    2.

    Wade and float through rain-fed lagoons at the height of the season, when the contrast between sand and water is at its most dramatic.

  2. Guided Dune Walks
    3.

    Explore the park with expert local guides who read the landscape like a map, revealing hidden lagoons and the stories behind them.

  3. Wind & Water in Atins
    4.

    In the laid-back village of Atins, kitesurfers dance across the horizon while hammocks sway under palm trees. Even if you never step on a board, the energy is addictive.

  4. River Journeys
    5.

    Travel along the Preguiças River from Barreirinhas, passing mangroves, small fishing communities, and slow, sun-soaked life along the banks.

Food & Flavour

Meals are simple, fresh, and deeply tied to place: grilled fish caught that day, rice and beans done perfectly, tropical fruits you’ll struggle to name but never forget. Dining here feels honest and generous without being showy.

When to go!

Best season: June to September, when lagoons are full
Quieter magic: Early June or late September, fewer visitors, same wonder
Dry season: Still beautiful, but with fewer lagoons and more about scale and solitude
